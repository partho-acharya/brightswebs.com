What is CAGR?

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) is the annualized growth rate of an investment over a specified time period, assuming profits are reinvested each year.

CAGR = (EV/BV)1/n - 1
EV = Ending Value BV = Beginning Value n = Number of years

How CAGR Calculation Works

Measures smoothed annual growth

Accounts for compounding effects

Ignores volatility between years

CAGR Percentage Calculation

Calculation Precision

  • 2 Decimal Places display
  • Up to 1012 value support
  • 0.01% minimum increment
CAGR = [(EV/BV)1/n - 1] × 100
EV = Final Value BV = Initial Value n = Years

Example Calculation

₹10,000 → ₹19,500 5 Years
(19500/10000)1/5 = 1.1428
1.1428 - 1 = 0.1428
0.1428 × 100 = 14.28%
